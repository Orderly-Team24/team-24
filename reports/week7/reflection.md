## Week 7 Reflection

### Learning points

**Follow-up maintenance**

- Learned that fixing bugs found during the customer meeting requires rapid response and prioritization over any other work.
- Understood that more thorough pre-release testing (including database schema validation and edge case flows) would have caught these issues before the demo.

**Final transition work**

- Learned that handover documentation (`customer-handover.md`, `CONTRIBUTING.md`, `AGENTS.md`) needs to be clear enough for the customer to run the app independently without team support.
- Learned that a full system video demonstration is a critical handover artifact, it provides the customer with a reference for independent operation and builds confidence in the product.

**Customer usefulness feedback**

- Confirmed that the customer will actually use the app themselves, which validates that the product solves a real problem.
- Validated that dietary preferences and allergy filtering features are essential for real-world usability and safety.
- Understood that customer approval of the sprint goal and handover documents is meaningful only if followed by actual independent use.

**Final delivery of MVP v3**

- Learned that delivering the last sprint is not just about completing tasks—it's about ensuring everything works together seamlessly.
- Validated that order history and dislike functionality meet customer expectations and are usable.
- Confirmed that dietary preferences text field (vegan, halal, etc.) works as expected and integrates properly with the AI recommendations.
- Learned that mobile layout fixes are essential for real-world usage.
- Understood that even minor bugs (like the history page failure) can undermine the perception of a polished, production-ready product.

### Validated assumptions

- Confirmed that the product is ready for independent customer use after remaining fixes are completed.
- Confirmed that customer UAT is the most effective way to find real usability and integration issues.
- Validated that the team can successfully deliver a complete MVP with all core features implemented.
- Confirmed that the AI recommendations with allergy filtering and dietary preferences meet safety requirements.

### Friction and gaps

- Internal testing did not catch the history page failure, which delayed final acceptance.

### Planned response

- Complete all remaining fixes immediately.
- Prepare and send a full system video demonstration to the customer.
- Finalize and transfer all handover documentation and repository ownership.
- Request written confirmation of acceptance from the customer.
- Apply lessons learned to future projects.
